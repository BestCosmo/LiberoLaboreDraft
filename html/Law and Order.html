The Punative System:

In Libero Labore prison will be completely rehabilitative. The Economist (LINK) said this - “Too many prisons make bad people worse.”. I think this quote is very accurate. For example, the USA the recidivism rate is 77%. Prison in the USA aims to scare criminals into not wanting to recommit. Although apparently, for 77% of criminals, prison isn’t ‘scary enough’. The dystopian London portrayed in George Orwell’s 1984 also uses this scaring strategy on their ‘thought criminals. Although unlike our world, it seems to be very effective. I would argue that this is because the main scaring strategies employed in 1984 is torture. My point being, for prisons that use scare strategies to properly decrees recidivism rates, they need to torture their inmates. Yet, in the vast majority of countries in our world, torture is illegal. So how can our current prison system ever be effective? “We punish them by taking away their freedom, but we don’t take away their life” – Correction officer, Bastoy prison, Norway. The Norwegian recidivism rate is a very low 20%. This can most logically be attributed to their system of rehabilitation apposed to punishment. Where in USA prisons, inmates do hard labour and are trapped in a cell, in Bastoy prison inmates have their own room with a tv, a recording studio for everyone to use, 5 star cooking classes, the list goes on. And so, the punitive system in Libero Labore wont be punitive at all. It will be 100% rehabilitative. Not to say caution wont be taken with those who are dangerous, just that those people won’t be treated inhumanly like in the USA.
